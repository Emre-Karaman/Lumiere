Un Helva: Aysegul Karaman

Ingredients:

  1. Wheat Flour
  2. Oil
  3. Sugar
  4. Nuts (Optional)
  5. Ice cream

Instructions:

  1. Mix the sugar and water in a small saucepan over high heat. Once it starts to boil, turn off the heat and set aside to let cool.
  2. Sifting the flour is important to minimize the chances of clumping. Sift a few times for best results. Heat the neutral oil (or melt the vegan butter) on a large, wide pan on low heat. Gradually add sifted flour while vigorously mixing with a wooden spoon or heatproof spatula. It will become smooth within a minute.

Sutlac/Rice Pudding: Gurlseren Celikdis

Ingredients:

  1. 50 grams short grain rice
  2. 110 millilitres water just enough to cover
  3. 80 grams sugar
  4. 450 millilitres fresh whole milk
  5. 100 millilitres double cream
  6. 1 dessert spoonful cornstarch
  7. 1 dessert spoonful vanilla extract or 1 sachet of vanilla sugar
  8. 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon to garnish

Instructions

  1. Add your water and rice to a medium-sized saucepan. Bring the water to the boil and then simmer until the water has been absorbed and the rice is cooked.
  2. Now add the milk, leaving a splash (around 30 mls) behind. Pour in the sugar and the double cream and stir together. Bring the mixture to the boil.
  3. Meanwhile, add the remainder of your milk to your cornstarch and stir until the cornstarch is dissolved. Turn the heat down to medium and gradually pour in your cornstarch and milk mixture, stirring constantly. Continue to stir your sütlaç gently for around 15 minutes until the pudding is thick and creamy.
  4. Share your sütlaç between 4 small, individual oven-proof bowls, dust the tops with a little cinnamon (if using) and allow to cool a little so that it sets on top. Preheat your grill and place your bowls of sütlaç under the heat until the top bubbles and darkens.
  5. Allow to cool and then place in the fridge for a couple of hours. Eat your sütlaç directly from the dish.

Gullac: Yesim Emecen

Ingredients:

  1. gullac sheets
  2. milk
  3. sugar
  4. crumbled walnuts
  5. ground pistachio and pomegranate arils for garnish

Instructions

  1. Put milk and sugar (and 1 tablespoon rosewater if you like) in a pot and heat it until the sugar melts. Stir it occasionally. It shouldn't be too hot to touch, so let it cool a little.
  2. Make the layers in a pan. Put a gullac sheet in the pan. Wet it with the warm milk. Repeat this with five sheets.
  3. After the fifth one, spread the crumbled walnut on it. Then put the rest five sheets one by one and wet each of them with milk. When you finish with the tenth one, pour the rest of the milk on it. They don't need to be very good in shape while arranging them in the pan.
  4. They will combine when they get wet with milk. After pouring the milk, you will see the sheets are rising, do not touch them. Cover it with stretch film and put it in refrigerator for at least 2 hours. Then serve and garnish with walnuts or pistachio and pomegranate

Turkish Milk Pudding: Gulcin Karaman

Ingredients:

  1. ¾ cup flour
  2. 1 ½ cup sugar
  3. 125-150 gr butter/margarine
  4. 1 lt milk
  5. 1 tsp vanilla extract/1 package vanilla powder
  6. 1 tbsp starch

Instructions:

  1. Melt the butter/margarine in a pot and then sauté flour and starch till the mixture gets yellow (about 2-3 minutes over medium heat). Add milk and sugar. Stir continuously to prevent sticking to the bottom. Bring to a boil and then reduce heat and cook until it gets thick (3-5 minutes).
  2. Then stir in vanilla and turn the heat off. Mix it with a blender or mixer for about 2-3 minutes to obtain a thick and nice consistency.
  3. Pour the Milk Pudding into a Pyrex Dish and cover. Leave it in the fridge for 5-6 hours to cool.
  4. Before serving you can sprinkle cinnamon cut it into square pieces and serve.
