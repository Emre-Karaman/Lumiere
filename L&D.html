Lunch & Dinner Recipies

Kofte: Aysegul Karaman

Ingredients:

  1. 500gr. ground meat
  2. 2 medium onions
  3. 4 tbsp ground stale bread/bread crumbs
  4. Handful of parsley (optional)
  5. 1 tbsp each of salt, pepper, and paprika
  6. Olive Oil

Instructions:

  1. In a food proccessor cut the parsley and onion then add the beef and all of the seasoning. Make sure it doesn't become a paste and still has some chunks
  2. When forming the kofte you want to make your hands a bit wet so that it doens't stick to your hands. You want to form each kofte into a ball then press down to make it a disk. The meat will come together and shrink while cooking so take that into consideration when forming the kofte pieces.
  3. Cook the kofte on a hot non-stick or castiron pan and apply olive oil to the top before adding the meat. The meat should form a crust if cooked correctly, and you want to take it off the pan when it has browned all around.
  4. Serve it with rice, vegetables, or yogurt.

Oven Baked Zuchinni: Gulcin Karaman

Ingredients:

  1. Zuchinni
  2. yogurt
  3. Garlic
  4. Olive Oil
  5. Salt

Instructions:

  1. Cut the Zuchinni into 1-2cm slices for the perfect size. Use as many Zuchinnis as you want and place them evenly on a oven tray and make sure to keep some space between all the Zuchinni.
  2. Set the oven to 200 ℃ - 390 ℉ for around an hour till the Zuchinni is cooked.
  3. While the zuchinni is cooking you want to very thinly dice the garlic and put it into the yogurt and mix it.
  4. Once everything is ready you can plate the zuchinni with as much yogurt as you want on top of it and then add salt at the end.

Manti: Yesim Emecen

Ingredients

  1. Yogurt
  2. Garlic
  3. Dried mint
  4. Sumac
  5. Chili flakes
  6. Olive Oil
  7. flour
  8. Salt and Pepper
  9. Onions
  10. Parsley
  11. Eggs
  12. Meat filling
  13. Water

Instructions:

  1. Dough: By combining flour, eggs, salt and water until everything holds together. Knead it for 5-10 minutes. It won't be a very soft dough. We make the dough with our hands, but you can use a food processor or a stand mixer if you want. Cover the dough ball with a damp kitchen towel and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Filling: In a medium bowl, combine ground meat, finely chopped onions, parsley, salt and pepper.
  3. Rolling the Dough: with a rolling pin. It doesn't have to be very thin. You can use a pasta roller for this step if you have one. Make it about 2mm or 1/16 inches. Using a sharp knife or a pizza cutter, cut it first into strips and then into small squares.
  4. Shapping Each Manti: Put a little filling on each square and close them up one by one to give each a bundle shape. How? As you see in the pictures below, first fold the tiny square into two and seal all the opposite corners of the dough in the center. Then you have this bundle-like shape. It takes us no longer than 20 minutes to finish them all!
  5. Cooking: Cook just like paste boiling water in a big pot and adding all the manti you have prepared. While the manti is cooking in a very small pan you add olive oil and chili flakes and cook until hot and almost boiling
  6. Sauces: While the manti is cooking in a very small pan you add olive oil and chili flakes and cook until hot and almost boiling mix and prepare the yogurt sauce by mixing the yogurt with finely diced garlic
  7. Serve: Serve the manti in a big bowl you can add pasta water to make it a watery or you can just add the manti itself its up to your own preference. To the manti you want to add the yogurt, and the chili oil, as well as the dried mint and sumac.

Kuru Fasulye/ Turkish Beans: Gulseren Celikdis

Ingredients:

  1. 400g of haricot beans
  2. 1 large onion finely diced
  3. 1 large tomato roughly chopped
  4. 1 tablespoon tomato puree
  5. 1 knob butter enough to cover bottom of pan
  6. salt and pepper to season
  7. 500 millilitres meat stock or hot water

Instructions:

  1. In a deep saucepan, melt your butter over a medium heat and add your onions. Sweat the onions until they start to become translucent and start to soften.
  2. Add your tomato and stir for a few minutes until it starts to break down. Add your tomato puree, salt and pepper and stir into the mixture.
  3. Now turn the heat to high, pour your water or stock into the pan and stir. Bring to the boil, cover and reduce to a simmer for 15-20 minutes until the stew is starting to thicken and the lamb has softened.
  4. Now add your haricot beans (kuru fasulye), stir and simmer for a further 10 minutes. Leave to rest for around 10 minutes and then serve.

Fasulye Diblesi Recipe: Aysegul Karaman

Ingredients:

  1. 500 g of green beans, chopped
  2. 1 large onion, chopped
  3. 1/2 cup rice
  4. 2 tomatoes, chopped
  5. 4-5 tablespoons of oil
  6. Water
  7. Oil
  8. Salt and Pepper

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in large pan, add the chopped onions till translucent, then add the beans and tomatoes and stir well
  2. Cover the lid and cook until it changes color. Add 2 cups of hot water and simmer over low heat until fully softened, Wash and drain the rice
  3. Add rice and 1 cup of boiling water, then cook till the rice is tender and all the water is gone from the pot.
